New Mom, New Career: Becoming a Coder

September 21, 2021

Last November, when the entire world was shut down and I could hardly visit my friends and family, something magical happened. I became a mom.
It's something I had wanted forever, yet still I never imagined the impact this beautiful, helpless babygirl would have on my life, on my desires, on my independence.
It became clear in a few short weeks that I could not return to my profession as a dental assistant; I had a child that needed me, and I needed to be with her.
Fast-forward 10 months and with an abundance of self-exploration, (no, not that kind you pervert) and I have found myself spending my evenings learning font-end web development. But I like to just call it coding...because it sounds much cooler.
I am hoping in a couple months that I don't have to "sound cool" because I will actually know wtf I am talking about. Wish me luck!

The Final Push: Why Coding?

September 22, 2021

So how did I end up choosing coding? It's quite a drastic leap from healthcare, right? I know. And I was/am scared. But I am also up for the challenge.(I'll do anything to be able to spend more time with my little girl!!)
There are several factors I had in mind for an ideal career change as a new mom

  1. Flexibility- I wanted to be able to work where and when I want
  2. Job Outlook- Okay, we know this job isn't going anywhere, and the demand just keeps growing
  3. Opportunity: I happen to have cousins with there own web-page business, who acutally want to train and hire me! They work out of there home, they're funny, super-talented, and great to work with!
    I literally prayed for this.

So why am I writing a blog you ask? Shouldn't I be spending the few hours of free time I have in the evenings to learn code?
Aha! The best way to learn is to do and I just happen to be learning as we speak....
